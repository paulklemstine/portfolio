## 🏆 Major Discoveries

### Discovery 1: The All-Right Path Produces Consecutive Factorizations

The "All-Right" path (always choosing M₃) produces a beautiful pattern:

| Depth | Triple | Odd Leg | Factorization | Euclid (m,n) |
|-------|--------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| 0 | (3,4,5) | 3 | 1×3 | (2,1) |
| 1 | (15,8,17) | 15 | 3×5 | (4,1) |
| 2 | (35,12,37) | 35 | 5×7 | (6,1) |
| 3 | (63,16,65) | 63 | 7×9 | (8,1) |
| 4 | (99,20,101) | 99 | 9×11 | (10,1) |
| 5 | (143,24,145) | 143 | 11×13 | (12,1) |

**CLOSED-FORM FORMULA** (machine-verified in Lean):
- Euclid params: m = 2(k+1), n = 1
- Odd leg: (2k+1)(2k+3) — product of consecutive odd numbers
- Even leg: 4(k+1)
- Hypotenuse: 4(k+1)² + 1
- Stereographic param: t = 1/(2k+3) → 1 as k → ∞

**Factoring consequence**: To factor N using only the all-right path, we check if N is divisible by any (2k+1)(2k+3). This is equivalent to trial division by consecutive odd numbers, but encoded geometrically in the tree!

### Discovery 2: The Silver Ratio and Pell's Equation

The "All-Mid" path converges to t = √2 - 1 ≈ 0.41421356...

This is the **silver ratio** (silver mean). The convergence is connected to Pell's equation x² - 2y² = ±1, with the Pell solutions appearing as the ratios of the legs:

| Depth | a/b | |a/b - 1| | Pell connection |
|-------|-----|----------|-----------------|
| 0 | 3/4 = 0.75 | 0.25 | |
| 1 | 21/20 = 1.05 | 0.05 | (3,2) → 3² - 2·2² = 1 |
| 2 | 119/120 ≈ 0.992 | 0.008 | (7,5) → 7² - 2·5² = -1 |
| 3 | 697/696 ≈ 1.001 | 0.001 | (17,12) → 17² - 2·12² = 1 |

**Machine-verified**: The Pell recurrence (x,y) → (x+2y, x+y) preserves x² - 2y² with sign flip, and the double step (3x+4y, 2x+3y) preserves it with same sign.

### Discovery 5: The Conformal Factor as a Navigation Signal

The conformal factor λ(t) = 2/(1+t²) provides directional information:
- **Left child** always INCREASES λ (moving toward t=0, the "north pole")
- **Right child** always DECREASES λ (moving toward t=1)
- **Mid child** changes λ moderately

This creates a "potential field" on the tree that can supplement angular distance for navigation.

**Key Discovery**: M₂ has dominant eigenvalue 3 + 2√2 = (1+√2)², explaining why the mid path grows hypotenuses exponentially while Left and Right paths grow linearly. All three preserve the Lorentz form Q = diag(1,1,-1).

### Experiment: Random Walk vs Guided Search

| N | Random Walk (avg depth) | Guided Search (depth) | Winner |
|---|------------------------|----------------------|--------|
| 77 | 1.8 | 2 | Random! |
| 323 | 4.4 | 7 | Random! |
| 1763 | 12.7 | 19 | Random! |
| 9797 | 28.0 | 47 | Random! |

**Surprising Discovery**: For small-to-medium N, RANDOM walks outperform greedy angular heuristics! This is because factor density in the tree is high (~50-66% of nodes have legs sharing a factor with N). Random walks explore diversity while greedy search follows a single path. The beam search's advantage comes from its width, not its heuristic.
