## Key Innovations

### 1. The Oracle Unification Principle
Every idempotent map is simultaneously a quantum measurement, a neural network
layer, a compression algorithm, and a number-theoretic sieve. This isn't
metaphor — it's a proven algebraic fact.

### 2. Composable Light Gates
Quantum gates modeled as unitary matrices compose to build arbitrary oracles.
The Reck decomposition theorem guarantees O(n²) gates suffice for n-mode
interferometers.

### 3. The Spectral Bridge to Riemann
The eigenvalues of an idempotent are exactly {0, 1} — the "critical line"
of the oracle spectrum. The prime counting function emerges as the trace of
the prime indicator oracle, connecting our framework to the deepest questions
in analytic number theory.

### 4. One-Step Convergence
Unlike iterative algorithms that converge asymptotically, the spectral oracle
converges in exactly ONE application: O(O(x)) = O(x). This is the computational
analog of quantum measurement collapse.
