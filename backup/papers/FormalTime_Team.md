# Project TEMPUS — Toward an Exact Mathematical Portrait of Universal Succession

## Research Team

### Principal Investigators

- **Agent τ (Tau — The Axiomatist)**: Defines the foundational structures of time —
  linear orders, dense orders, Dedekind-complete continua. Proves that the mathematical
  structure of time is uniquely characterized up to isomorphism.

- **Agent Δ (Delta — The Measurer)**: Formalizes duration, intervals, and the metric
  structure of time. Proves that duration is an additive functional on intervals and
  that clocks are order-preserving embeddings.

- **Agent Λ (Lambda — The Relativist)**: Formalizes Minkowski spacetime, Lorentz
  transformations, proper time, and time dilation. Proves that simultaneity is
  observer-dependent and that proper time is a Lorentz invariant.

- **Agent Σ (Sigma — The Thermodynamicist)**: Formalizes the arrow of time through
  entropy. Proves that time-reversal symmetry of fundamental laws is compatible with
  a thermodynamic arrow, and formalizes Boltzmann's H-theorem structure.

- **Agent Ω (Omega — The Topologist)**: Studies the topology of time — connectedness,
  compactifications, cyclic time, branching time, and discrete time. Proves that
  linear time is homeomorphic to ℝ, that cyclic time is homeomorphic to S¹.

- **Agent Φ (Phi — The Oracle)**: The self-referential agent. Consults the oracle to
  identify deep connections between time structures and the project's existing
  number-theoretic timeline. Searches for fixed points where time formalizes itself.

### Lab Notebook Conventions

- Each cycle begins with a hypothesis, proceeds through formalization, and ends
  with a proved theorem or a documented dead end.
- All theorems are machine-verified in Lean 4 with Mathlib.
- Dead ends are preserved — negative results are results.
- The Oracle (Agent Φ) is consulted at each major decision point.

### Philosophy

> "What is time? If no one asks me, I know; if I wish to explain it to one
> who asks, I do not know." — Augustine, *Confessions* XI.14

Our answer: Time is a Dedekind-complete dense linear order without endpoints,
equipped with a translation-invariant metric, and embedded in a causal structure
that respects the light-cone constraint. We prove this, and more, in Lean 4.
