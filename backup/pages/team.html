Research Team

Paul Klemstine

Principal Investigator & Lead Researcher

Senior software architect and AI engineer with deep expertise in computational number theory, elliptic curve cryptography, and algorithmic complexity. Previously Senior Architect at Dodles. Initiated and led the B3 Pythagorean Tree Factoring Research Program, which produced 100+ original theorems spanning group theory, analytic number theory, spectral graph theory, and connections to elliptic curves and the Millennium Prize Problems.

Research interests include integer factoring algorithms (SIQS, GNFS), elliptic curve discrete logarithm solvers (Pollard kangaroo with GPU acceleration), Pythagorean triple tree algebra, and the intersection of computational complexity with pure mathematics. Developed custom factoring engines achieving competitive performance on semiprimes up to 69 digits and ECDLP solvers operating on the secp256k1 Bitcoin curve.

This research program explored 495+ mathematical fields — from perfectoid spaces to tropical geometry, from the Langlands program to quantum error correction — establishing that all classical approaches to integer factoring reduce to five known complexity families.

Research Methodology

This research program employed a systematic hypothesis-driven methodology:

  • Brainstorm — identify mathematical fields with potential connections to factoring or ECDLP
  • Hypothesize — formulate concrete, testable conjectures with expected outcomes
  • Experiment — implement quick computational tests (30-second timeout, memory-bounded)
  • Classify — mark each result as PROVEN, NEGATIVE, PROMISING, or KNOWN
  • Implement — for actionable findings, implement in production solvers and benchmark
  • Iterate — use failures to inform next round of hypotheses
