T19c: Complete Chomsky Classification of PPT Languages

Theorem (Chomsky Classification). PPT-related languages span the full Chomsky hierarchy:
  • Regular (Type 3): Berggren tree paths $\{A, B, C\}^*$ (free monoid, 1-state DFA)
  • NOT context-free: Hypotenuse set in unary $\{1^c\}$ (non-semilinear gaps by Parikh's theorem)
  • Context-sensitive (Type 1): Hypotenuse membership (LBA checks sum-of-squares, decidable in P)
  • Decidable (non-elementary): Full MSO theory of the tree (Rabin S3S, tower-of-exponentials complexity)

Intuition

The Chomsky hierarchy classifies formal languages by the computational power needed to recognize them. PPT objects naturally populate every level:
  • Tree paths are trivially regular — every finite string over $\{A,B,C\}$ is valid
  • Tree outputs (hypotenuses as numbers) require factoring to check, jumping to context-sensitive
  • The full theory of the ternary tree is decidable by Rabin's theorem but with non-elementary complexity
This is the first complete classification of PPT-related languages across all four Chomsky levels.

Proof Sketches

Not regular (binary): Hypotenuses include all primes $p \equiv 1 \bmod 4$. These have unbounded gaps among integers of the same bit-length, violating the periodicity requirement (Myhill-Nerode theorem).

Not context-free (unary): By Parikh's theorem, unary CF languages are eventually periodic (semilinear). Hypotenuse gaps show 5 distinct values in $[4, 20]$, inconsistent with eventual periodicity.

Context-sensitive: An LBA can check $c = m^2 + n^2$ with $\gcd(m,n) = 1$ in polynomial space. Membership is actually in P.

Significance

This classification reveals that PPT objects are maximally complex in the formal language sense: they populate every level of the Chomsky hierarchy. The proof-theoretic phase transition from $\mathrm{RCA}_0$ to $\Pi^1_1$-$\mathrm{CA}_0$ shows that the structure of PPTs is computationally trivial but the theory of PPTs requires the full power of second-order arithmetic.
