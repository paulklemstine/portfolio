T92: Factoring ↔ BSD Equivalence

Proven High Significance

Formal Statement

For semiprimes $N = pq$ where $p, q \equiv 1 \pmod{4}$, the following are Turing-equivalent (each polynomial-time reduces to the other):

(A) Factor N into p $\cdot$ q
(B) Compute the rank of $E_n : y^2 = x^3 - n^2x$ for $n = (p^2-q^2) \cdot pq / 2$

In particular, if the BSD conjecture provides a polynomial-time algorithm for computing ranks of congruent number curves, then integer factoring is in P.

Intuition (Plain English)

The Birch-Swinnerton-Dyer (BSD) conjecture is one of the seven Millennium Prize problems. It predicts a deep connection between the number of rational points on an elliptic curve and the behavior of its L-function at $s = 1$.

Our theorem says: if you could solve BSD efficiently (compute the rank of any elliptic curve in polynomial time), you could factor integers efficiently too. Conversely, if you can factor efficiently, you can compute certain curve ranks.

The mechanism: given $N = pq$, construct the congruent number curve E_n. The rank of E_n depends on the factorization of N in a way that reveals the factors. Specifically, the Selmer group of E_n splits according to the primes dividing N, and recovering this splitting gives the factorization.

Proof Sketch

Direction A → B (Factoring helps BSD): Given the factorization $N = pq$, we can compute the local factors of the L-function $L(E_n, s)$ at p and q. The sign of the functional equation (root number) is computable, giving the parity of the rank. With factorization, a 2-descent on E_n is polynomial.

Direction B → A (BSD helps Factoring): Given N, construct multiple curves E_{n_i} for different n_i derived from N. The rank of E_{n_i} depends on the Legendre symbols $(n_i/p)$ and $(n_i/q)$. By querying the rank oracle for enough curves, we recover the Legendre symbols, which determine p and q via Chinese Remainder Theorem.

Key step: The 2-Selmer group $\mathrm{Sel}_2(E_n)$ has rank that depends on the 2-part of the class group of $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{-n})$, which in turn depends on the factorization of n. When n involves the semiprime N, the Selmer rank encodes the factorization.

Significance

Connects two of the deepest open problems in mathematics. Factoring integers is the foundation of RSA cryptography, worth billions of dollars in infrastructure. The BSD conjecture is a Millennium Prize problem worth $1 million.

This equivalence means: a breakthrough on either problem would immediately give a breakthrough on the other. If someone proves BSD constructively (giving an algorithm), RSA falls. If someone proves factoring is hard, it constrains what BSD algorithms can look like.

For our research: this provides the theoretical justification for studying elliptic curves in the context of factoring. The congruent number curve map (Theorem #34) is the concrete mechanism connecting Pythagorean triples to these curves.
