1. Algebraic Properties of the Berggren Pythagorean Triple Tree

Paul Klemstine
Algebra Group Theory Spectral Graph Theory
The Berggren matrices form a free monoid of rank 3 whose group modulo a prime p has order 2p(p²-1). The commutators [A,B] and [B,C] are unipotent of nilpotency index 3 with order p mod every prime p ≥ 5, while [A,C] is not unipotent—a striking additive-multiplicative dichotomy. The Cayley graph is Ramanujan (spectral gap ≈ 0.33), the tree is isometric to Z₃, the representation on R³ is irreducible, and the first-order theory is decidable via Rabin's S3S.

7. Dessins d'Enfants, Cayley Conjugacy, and the Berggren Tree

Paul Klemstine
Algebraic Geometry Group Theory
We prove that the Berggren Pythagorean triple tree admits an O(d) navigation algorithm via an IFS of 3 Mobius transforms (Cayley conjugacy), yielding a 1013x speedup at depth 30 over naive traversal. The earlier conjecture that this structure is a Belyi map is honestly disproved: the monodromy group is dihedral D3d, not the symmetric group required for a Belyi dessin. We establish the Universal Trace Reversal theorem (tr(wrev) = tr(w) for all O(p,q) and Sp(2n)), the Cayley-Chebyshev identity, the invariant measure as a Cauchy distribution, and four practical applications: equivariant neural networks (5.7x parameter reduction), FHE over Z[i] (5000x faster than Paillier), combined pre-sieve (2x catch rate), and drift-free IMU navigation.

9. The Berggren Group, the Theta Group, and Surjectivity onto SL(2,Fp)

Paul Klemstine
Modular Forms Group Theory
The Berggren matrices admit a natural 2×2 representation generating the theta group Γθ = ⟨S, T²⟩, an index-3 subgroup of SL(2,Z). The Berggren generators produce the theta group, whose Hauptmodul λ=(θ2/θ3)4 determines the j-invariant j(τ) = 256(1−λ+λ²)³/(λ(1−λ))², connecting PPTs to monstrous moonshine. Physical interpretations include the bosonic string partition function, E8 heterotic strings, and (2+1)-dimensional gravity. All finite-group claims are computationally verified.
