Deep Dive #8

Inside-Out Factoring

The Berggren tree as an integer-factoring algorithm — with a provable energy descent landscape.

The IOF Algorithm

Inside-Out Factoring starts with a target semiprime N and constructs the starting Pythagorean triple:

(N,  (N² − 1)/2,  (N² + 1)/2)

Then iterates: at each step k, the leg evolves. When k = (p−1)/2 for a factor p, gcd(leg, N) > 1 reveals the factor.

Interactive Factoring Demo

Energy Descent

The IOF energy function E(k) = (N − 2k)² decreases monotonically — the algorithm always makes progress toward the factor.

E≥0 Energy Non-negative

E(k) = (N − 2k)² ≥ 0 always. The energy landscape is a parabola.

DROP Monotone Descent

E(k+1) < E(k) whenever N − 2k > 1. Energy decreases at every step.

GRAD Linear Gradient

Second difference = 8 (parabolic). The energy landscape is perfectly smooth.

PER Factor Periodicity

Factor steps form arithmetic progressions mod p — revealing algebraic structure.

CFRAC-Tree Equivalence

Two Algorithms, One Framework

The continued fraction algorithm for factoring is equivalent to a walk on the Pythagorean tree with adaptive step sizes M(a_k). This unifies two apparently different mathematical structures: Gauss's continued fractions and the Berggren ternary tree.
