Factoring ↔ BSD Equivalence (T92)

Perhaps the most profound result connecting pure math to computation: integer factoring and the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture are Turing-equivalent for semiprimes.

Factoring(N = pq) ≡_T BSD-rank(E_n)
If BSD provides a polynomial-time rank algorithm, then Integer Factoring ∈ P

The Connection

Every primitive Pythagorean triple (a,b,c) maps to a rational point on a congruent number elliptic curve E_n: y² = x³ − n²x. The Berggren tree generates an infinite family of congruent numbers with constructive witnesses. Since factoring semiprimes can be reduced to finding rational points (and vice versa), the two problems are computationally equivalent — connecting two Millennium Prize problems through the Pythagorean tree.
