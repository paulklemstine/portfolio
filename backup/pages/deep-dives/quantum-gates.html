Quantum Gate Synthesis

The Berggren gates form a free group inside SU(2), connecting the ancient Pythagorean tree to universal quantum computation.

The Berggren Gate Group

The Berggren matrices, when lifted to 2×2 form via their connection to SL(2,ℤ), become quantum gates. These gates are unitary, non-commutative, and generate a dense subgroup of SU(2).

Universality

CrystalBQP = BQP

The crystallized quantum gates achieve computational universality — CrystalBQP equals standard BQP. This means the Pythagorean framework can simulate any quantum computation, with the additional benefit of exact rational arithmetic.

Circuit Synthesis

Circuits built from Berggren gates have provable properties:

DET Determinant Preservation

Circuit evaluation preserves determinant. Every gate product has det = 1.

SIMP Circuit Simplification

B₁·B₂·B₁ = B₂ allows algebraic circuit optimization — reducing gate count.

FACT Factoring from Circuits

Circuit evaluation yields factorization: m²−n² = N ⟹ N = (m+n)(m−n).

DEPTH Depth Bounds

Gate count bounded by lattice rank — crystallized circuits have provable complexity bounds.
